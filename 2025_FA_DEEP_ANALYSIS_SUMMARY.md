### 2. PLATE DISCIPLINE SUSTAINABILITY ANALYSIS

**Method:** Evaluated sustainable skills (zone contact, chase rate, plate discipline) vs volatile outcomes (barrel rate, BABIP). Hypothesis: Elite discipline ages better than power-only approaches.

#### Player Archetypes

**Unicorns (Elite Discipline + Elite Power) - 4 players**
- Kyle Schwarber, Eugenio Suarez, Pete Alonso, Trent Grisham
- **Insight:** Rare combination. These age extremely well (discipline sustains when power fades)

**Grinders (Elite Discipline + Average Power) - 21 players**
- Kyle Tucker, Willy Adames, Cody Bellinger, Harrison Bader, etc.
- **Insight:** Safe bets. Discipline is more stable than power year-to-year
- 25 of 28 batters qualified as "safe bets" (elite discipline score 70+)

**Risky Sluggers (Poor Discipline + Power) - 0 players**
- None in this FA class met criteria
- **Insight:** 2025-26 class is unusually disciplined overall

**Below Average - 3 players**
- Not disclosed (poor overall profile)

#### Key Insight

**All top-tier free agents have elite plate discipline.** This is unusual - most FA classes have 5-10 "risky sluggers" who chase and whiff excessively. The 2025-26 class is exceptionally sustainable from a discipline perspective.
