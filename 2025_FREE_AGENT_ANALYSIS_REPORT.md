## Expected Stats Analysis Framework

### What Are Expected Stats?

Baseball Savant's **expected statistics (xStats)** use exit velocity, launch angle, and sprint speed to calculate what a player's results *should have been* based purely on batted ball quality:

- **xBA (expected batting average)** - Based on similar batted balls historically
- **xSLG (expected slugging)** - Based on exit velo + launch angle
- **xwOBA (expected weighted on-base average)** - Comprehensive contact quality metric

### The xStats Gap

**Gap = Expected Stat - Actual Stat**

- **Positive gap** (+0.020 or more) = Player was **unlucky**
  - Hit ball hard consistently but results didn't show it
  - BABIP luck, defensive positioning, sample randomness
  - **Buy-low candidate** - likely to improve without skill change

- **Negative gap** (-0.020 or more) = Player was **lucky**
  - Results exceeded quality of contact
  - Weak contact found holes, batted ball luck
  - **Regression risk** - likely to decline

### Quality of Contact Metrics

**Barrel Rate** - Optimal exit velo + launch angle combo
- Elite: 15%+
- Above avg: 10-15%
- Average: 7-10%
- Below avg: <7%

**Exit Velocity**
- Elite: 93+ mph average
- Above avg: 91-93 mph
- Average: 89-91 mph
- Below avg: <89 mph

**Hard-Hit Rate** - % of batted balls 95+ mph
- Elite: 50%+
- Above avg: 45-50%
- Average: 40-45%
- Below avg: <40%

## Market Inefficiencies to Exploit

### 1. Traditional Stats vs xStats Gap

**Inefficiency:** Most teams still overweight traditional stats (BA, RBI, ERA) vs expected stats.

**Opportunity:** Find players with:
- Low BA but high xBA (unlucky BABIP)
- Low RBI but high barrel rate (lineup/opportunity dependent)
- High ERA but good xFIP/xERA (poor defense/sequencing)

**Example archetype:** Player with .245/.330/.450 slash but:
- xBA .270
- Barrel rate 12% (elite)
- Exit velo 92 mph
- BABIP .260 (30 points below league average)

**Market value:** $18M AAV based on traditional stats
**True value:** $26M AAV based on expected stats and contact quality
**Surplus opportunity:** $8M annually

---

### 2. Age Cliff Timing

**Inefficiency:** Teams sign 32-33 year olds to 6-7 year deals despite aging curve data showing cliff at 33-35.

**Opportunity:** Target players age 28-30 on longer deals. Avoid 33+ on deals beyond 4 years.

**Example:**
- **Bad contract:** Alex Bregman 7yr/$275M (age 32-39) - Years 5-7 will be disaster
- **Good contract:** Gleyber Torres 6yr/$125M (age 29-34) - Ages 29-32 are prime years

**Surplus created:** Torres provides 24 WAR over 6 years ($192M value at $8M/WAR) for $125M cost = **$67M surplus**

### 3. Positional Value Premiums

**Inefficiency:** Market undervalues premium defensive positions (SS, CF, C) and overvalues offense-only positions (1B, DH).

**Opportunity:**
- Pay premium for elite defenders at SS/CF/C
- Avoid overpaying for 1B/DH with poor defense

**Example:**
- Willy Adames (SS, elite defense) 7yr/$180M = $26M AAV
- Pete Alonso (1B, poor defense) 5yr/$150M = $30M AAV

**Value:** Adames provides defensive WAR (15-20 runs annually) that Alonso doesn't. If offense is equal, Adames is worth $5M+ more per year.

### 4. Reliever Volatility

**Inefficiency:** Teams sign relievers to 4-5 year deals despite 10% annual decline rate and high volatility.

**Opportunity:** Avoid multi-year reliever deals beyond 3 years. Stream relievers on 1-2 year deals.

**Example:**
- **Bad approach:** Pay closer $18M AAV √ó 5 years = $90M
- **Good approach:** Sign 3 relievers at $6M AAV √ó 2 years, replace as needed

**Risk mitigation:** If one reliever flames out, you're only on hook for 1-2 years, not 5.

### 5. Starting Pitcher Injury Risk

**Inefficiency:** Teams give long-term deals to pitchers with injury history or high innings loads.

**Opportunity:**
- Target pitchers with clean injury history
- Include innings limits and performance incentives
- Avoid SP over age 33 on 5+ year deals

**Red flags:**
- Previous Tommy John surgery
- 200+ innings in 3 consecutive years (fatigue risk)
- Velocity decline of 2+ mph
- Age 34+ (sharp decline ahead)

**Example:**
- **Avoid:** Blake Snell 6yr/$180M (age 32, injury history) - Years 4-6 disaster risk
- **Target:** Ranger Suarez 6yr/$195M (age 30, clean health, improving) - Safer bet

## Risk Management Strategies

### Contract Structure Best Practices

#### 1. Opt-Outs (Player and Team)

**Player opt-outs** (after years 2-3):
- **Pro:** If player exceeds expectations, they opt out (you already got surplus value)
- **Con:** If player declines, they stay (you're stuck)
- **Use case:** High-upside signings where you win if they perform well

**Team opt-outs** (after years 3-4):
- **Pro:** If player declines, you can cut losses
- **Con:** Hard to negotiate, players demand higher AAV
- **Use case:** Aging players (32+) where cliff risk is high

**Example:** Alex Bregman 6yr/$240M with player opt-out after year 3
- Years 1-3 at age 32-34: Likely productive
- If healthy/productive: He opts out, you got 3 elite years
- If declining: He stays, but you got 3 good years + 3 bad (net positive)

---

#### 2. Performance Incentives

**Structure:** Base salary + incentives for games played, WAR thresholds, awards

**Example:**
- Base: $20M AAV
- Incentives: +$3M for 150+ games, +$2M for 4+ WAR, +$2M for All-Star
- Actual pay: $20-27M depending on performance

**Benefit:** Align player incentives with team goals, reduce risk if player declines

---

#### 3. Deferred Money

**Structure:** Pay portion of contract 5-10 years after signing

**Example:** 10yr/$300M contract with $100M deferred
- Annual cash outlay: $20M
- Actual total obligation: $300M (but reduced present value)

**Benefit:** Reduces present-day payroll burden, allows more flexibility

**Risk:** Still on hook if player flames out (see Bobby Bonilla)

---

#### 4. Front-Loading vs Back-Loading

**Front-load** (pay more in early years):
- **Use for:** Contending teams who need production NOW
- **Benefit:** Easier to trade player later with lower salary
- **Example:** 6yr deal - $40M, $35M, $30M, $25M, $20M, $20M = $170M total

**Back-load** (pay more in later years):
- **Use for:** Rebuilding teams managing current payroll
- **Benefit:** Lower early payroll impact
- **Risk:** Player declining in high-salary years
- **Example:** 6yr deal - $20M, $20M, $25M, $30M, $35M, $40M = $170M total

### Injury Risk Mitigation

#### Red Flags to Avoid

1. **Previous Tommy John surgery** (SP/RP)
   - 25-30% chance of re-injury
   - Velocity often doesn't fully return
   - Limit to 4-5 year deals maximum

2. **Multiple IL stints** (any position)
   - Player with 3+ IL stints in past 3 years = high risk
   - Include games played incentives

3. **Age 32+ with declining exit velo** (hitters)
   - 2+ mph exit velo drop = skill erosion
   - Avoid deals longer than 3-4 years

4. **High innings workload** (SP)
   - 200+ IP in 4+ consecutive years = fatigue risk
   - Monitor for velocity decline, injury spike in year 5-6

#### Protective Contract Clauses

- **Games played minimums** - Player must play 120+ games to earn full salary
- **Medical reviews** - Annual physicals with team doctors
- **Rehab assignment terms** - Clear timeline for return from injury
- **Voided options** - If player misses 100+ games due to injury, team options void
