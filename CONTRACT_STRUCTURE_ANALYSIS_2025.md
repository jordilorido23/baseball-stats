## Executive Summary

Traditional free agent analysis focuses on total contract value and $/WAR metrics. This analysis demonstrates that **contract structure can be worth $50-350M in real value** depending on deferrals, opt-outs, and incentive structures.

### Key Findings

1. **Deferred Money Creates Massive Savings**
   - Shohei Ohtani's $700M contract costs Dodgers $355M in present value (5% discount rate)
   - **$345M savings** that can be reinvested immediately
   - CBT (luxury tax) savings: $34.5M per year

2. **Opt-Outs Significantly Reduce Team Control**
   - Elite players (8+ WAR, age <32) have 60-100% probability of exercising opt-outs
   - Teams lose 2-4 expected years of control
   - Must pay 10-15% premium to include opt-out clauses

3. **Performance Bonuses Create Value for Both Sides**
   - Optimal structures save teams 20-40% of bonus pool in expectation
   - Players get meaningful upside for elite performance
   - Best thresholds: 75-85th percentile of expected performance

4. **Structure Matters as Much as Total Value**
   - Same player, different structures = $30-80M NPV difference
   - CBT optimization creates $50M+ roster flexibility
   - Risk allocation dramatically changes true contract value

## Performance Bonus Optimization

### Methodology

Optimal bonus structures balance:
1. **Team Risk:** Minimize probability of overpaying
2. **Player Upside:** Meaningful reward for elite performance
3. **Fairness:** Thresholds achievable but not guaranteed

**Expected Performance:** 650 PA, 80 PA standard deviation
**Bonus Pool:** $20M

| Tier | PA Threshold | Bonus | Probability | Expected Value |
|------|--------------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | 550 PA | $8M | 89% | $7.2M |
| 2 | 600 PA | $6M | 73% | $4.4M |

**Total Expected Payout:** $14.1M

**Interpretation:**
- Tier 1 (550 PA): Easy to hit unless injured - reasonable baseline
- Tier 3 (650 PA): Exactly expected value - 50/50 shot
- Tier 4 (700 PA): Stretch goal - elite performance needed

### Dylan Cease Example (Pitcher)

**Expected Performance:** 180 IP, 25 IP standard deviation
**Bonus Pool:** $15M

| Tier | IP Threshold | Bonus | Probability | Expected Value |
|------|--------------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | 160 IP | $6M | 79% | $4.7M |
| 2 | 180 IP | $4.5M | 50% | $2.3M |
| 3 | 200 IP | $3M | 21% | $0.6M |
| 4 | 220 IP | $1.5M | 6% | $0.1M |

**Total Expected Payout:** $7.7M
**Team Savings:** $7.3M (49%)

**Interpretation:**
- Pitchers have higher injury risk → larger team savings expected
- 200+ IP increasingly rare in modern baseball → big bonus for durability
- Teams save ~50% of bonus pool in expectation (vs 30% for batters)

### Bonus Structure Recommendations

**For Position Players:**
- Use PA thresholds (not games played - games can be empty stats)
- Target 60th, 75th, 85th, 95th percentile of projection
- Expected payout: 70-80% of pool (teams save 20-30%)

**For Pitchers:**
- Use IP thresholds (not starts - incentivizes going deep)
- Target 65th, 80th, 90th, 98th percentile of projection
- Expected payout: 50-60% of pool (teams save 40-50% due to injury risk)

**For Both:**
- Avoid outcome-based incentives (WAR, ERA+) - prohibited by CBA anyway
- Avoid "all-or-nothing" structures - create perverse incentives
- Use 3-5 tiers for smooth progression

## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception #1: "Deferred money is just accounting tricks"

**Reality:** Deferred money creates REAL value through:
1. **Time value of money** - $68M in 2034 ≠ $68M in 2024
2. **Investment opportunity** - Money not paid now can be invested
3. **Inflation** - Future dollars buy less than today's dollars
4. **CBT optimization** - Creates roster flexibility NOW

**Example:** Ohtani's $700M costs Dodgers $355M NPV - this is $345M they can spend on other players TODAY.

### Misconception #2: "Opt-outs favor players so teams should avoid them"

**Reality:** Depends entirely on player age and position:
- **Age 32+ position players:** 70-100% opt-out probability → AVOID
- **Age 28-30 position players:** 20-60% opt-out probability → NEUTRAL (depends on premium)
- **Pitchers any age:** 1-15% opt-out probability → TEAM-FRIENDLY (lower AAV for minimal risk)

**Example:** Dylan Cease with opt-out after Year 5 has only 1% chance of exercising. Team gets "discount" on AAV for virtually zero risk.

### Misconception #3: "Performance bonuses are team-friendly"

**Reality:** Well-structured bonuses are WIN-WIN:
- **Teams:** Save 20-50% of bonus pool in expectation (players don't always hit targets)
- **Players:** Get upside for elite performance
- **Both:** Aligns incentives (durability, availability)

**Bad Structure:** $10M bonus for 4.0+ WAR (outcome-based, prohibited by CBA)
**Good Structure:** $10M in tiers for 550/600/650 PA (playing time-based, legal and fair)

### Misconception #4: "NPV doesn't matter because teams have money"

**Reality:** NPV creates ROSTER flexibility:
- **Luxury Tax:** Calculated on present value, not stated value
- **Roster Slots:** $30M in CBT savings = can sign another player
- **Opportunity Cost:** Money not spent on Ohtani can be spent on Judge

**Example:** Dodgers' Ohtani savings ($34.5M/year CBT space) allowed them to later sign other stars within luxury tax.

## Appendix: Methodology

### NPV Calculation

```
NPV = Σ (Payment_t / (1 + r)^t)
```

Where:
- `Payment_t` = payment in year t
- `r` = discount rate (5% used in analysis)
- `t` = years from present

### Opt-Out Monte Carlo Simulation

**Variables Simulated (10,000 iterations):**
- WAR performance: Normal(μ = current WAR × decline^year, σ = 1.5)
- Injury: 15% annual probability of 50% performance decline
- Market $/WAR inflation: 5% annually

**Opt-Out Decision:**
Player opts out if:
```
Projected New Contract Value > Remaining Guarantee × 1.10
```

(Requires 10% premium to account for risk of injury/decline)

### Performance Bonus Optimization

**Probability Calculation:**
```
P(exceeding threshold) = 1 - Φ((threshold - μ) / σ)
```

Where:
- Φ = Standard normal CDF
- μ = expected PA/IP
- σ = standard deviation

**Expected Value:**
```
EV = Σ (Bonus_i × Probability_i)
```
