**Here's the controversy:** Articles cite Naylor's 39.8% O-Swing% (chase rate) as a red flag for his contract value. They're not wrong - but they're missing critical context.

**The Result:**
- Only strikes out 13.7% of the time (elite)
- Hits .295 with solid production
- Creates offense despite poor pitch selection

### Player Classification: "Elite Contact Hitter with Aggressive Approach"

Naylor is NOT a traditional "grinder" with elite discipline. Better classification: **Vladimir Guerrero Jr type** - aggressive hitter who succeeds through elite contact ability rather than patience.

**Why This Profile Can Work:**

1. **Contact skills are more stable** than power year-to-year
2. **Low strikeout rates (13.7%)** age exceptionally well
3. **Makes contact on bad pitches** - a rare skill that doesn't require patience
4. **Less vulnerable to velocity** compared to patient hitters who take strikes
5. **Historically successful** when paired with good contact (Vlad Jr, Jose Ramirez, Tony Gwynn)
